### 5. Kingdom Historical Dates & Disputed Facts

| Kingdom | Current Period in App | Known Issue |
|---------|----------------------|-------------|
| Sriwijaya | 650–1377 CE | End date disputed — after Chola raids (1025 CE) Sriwijaya declined; 1377 is a very late date. Research consensus. |
| Mataram Kuno | 717–1006 CE | Start year varies: some sources say 716 CE or 732 CE. The 732 CE Canggal inscription is often cited. |
| Medang Kemulan | 850–1006 CE | "Medang Kemulan" is a simplified popular name. Proper name: Kerajaan Medang or Mataram Medang. |
| Kahuripan | 1006–1045 CE | Founding year after collapse of Medang is disputed (some say 1009 CE). Verify against Airlangga's reign dates. |
| Demak | 1475–1554 CE | Some sources say founded 1475, others 1481. The 1475 CE estimate is connected to the Wali Songo tradition. |
| Pajang | 1549–1587 CE | The centroid data shows "1568–1586 CE" but map.tsx shows "1549–1587 CE". Reconcile — use 1568 as founding year. |
| Ken Arok / Singhasari | — | Ken Arok is part historical figure, part legend (Pararaton). Note this distinction in the app description. |

### 6. Mythology Creatures — Fact-Check & Additions

**Current 4 in app — verify these descriptions:**
- **Garuda** — "Sacred mount of Vishnu" ✓ accurate; also national symbol of Indonesia
- **Nyai Roro Kidul** — "Ruler of the Southern Ocean" — correct term is Samudra Hindia (Indian Ocean/Southern Sea); she is associated with green color — add this detail
- **Barong** — "Protector of Bali" ✓ correct; he is the king of spirits (Bhatara), eternal enemy of Rangda
- **Kuntilanak** — "Wandering forest spirit" ✗ **incorrect** — she is specifically the spirit of a woman who died in childbirth; not a generic forest spirit. Rewrite description.

**Research these 8 additions:**
For each, find: correct role/type, iconic physical attributes, which culture/island, one concise description, and any connection to a kingdom.

1. **Rangda** — demon queen of Bali; enemy of Barong; widow/witch figure
2. **Leak** (Leyak) — Balinese shapeshifting sorcerer; can transform into animals or floating head
3. **Naga** — divine serpent; appears in Javanese and Balinese mythology, associated with water and fertility
4. **Genderuwo** (Gendruwo) — large hairy male spirit; distinct from Kuntilanak; associated with old trees
5. **Semar** — clown-god of Javanese wayang; one of the most revered figures; represents common folk's wisdom
6. **Dewi Sri** — goddess of rice and fertility; origin story explains rice cultivation in Java and Bali
7. **Roro Jonggrang** — the legend connected to Prambanan temple; princess who was turned to stone
8. **Bima** — the second Pandawa warrior; son of the wind god; associated with strength and loyalty

### 7. Trivia Questions — Fact-Check

**Three answers to double-check:**
1. "Which of these is NOT one of the Pandawa Five? Gatotkaca" — **Correct**, but note: Gatotkaca is Bima's son, which makes this trick question confusing. Consider rewriting.
2. "The Sriwijaya empire was centered in Palembang" — **Mostly correct**, but there is ongoing academic debate. Some scholars argue it was Jambi. The app's answer of Palembang is the mainstream view.
3. "The first king of Singhasari was Ken Arok" — **Correct historically**, but Ken Arok's story in the Pararaton contains legendary embellishments. A footnote might help.

### 11. Google OAuth — Production Setup

To enable real Google Sign-In (currently shows Alert in dev):

1. Go to https://console.cloud.google.com → Create project "Nusantara Journey"
2. Enable "Google People API" and "Google Sign-In"
3. Go to Credentials → Create OAuth 2.0 Client IDs:
   - **Web client** → set Authorized redirect URIs to `https://auth.expo.io/@<expo-username>/nusantara-journey`
   - **iOS client** → set Bundle ID to `com.nusantarajourney.app`
   - **Android client** → provide SHA-1 fingerprint from your keystore
4. Set these environment variables:
   - `EXPO_PUBLIC_GOOGLE_WEB_CLIENT_ID`
   - `EXPO_PUBLIC_GOOGLE_IOS_CLIENT_ID`
   - `EXPO_PUBLIC_GOOGLE_ANDROID_CLIENT_ID`

## PART B — Real Problem Data for the PIDI Proposal

For each problem the app solves, here is the claim to make and where to find real supporting numbers.

---

### Problem 1: Heritage Tourism Revenue Gap

**Claim to make**: Indonesian cultural heritage sites generate significantly less revenue per visitor than comparable sites in Thailand and Vietnam.

**Where to research**:
- World Bank "Indonesia: Tourism for Growth and Jobs" (2023): https://www.worldbank.org/en/country/indonesia/publication/indonesia-tourism-for-growth-and-jobs
- UNWTO Asia-Pacific Tourism Trends: https://www.unwto.org/asia-pacific
- Kemenparekraf Annual Report (Laporan Tahunan): https://kemenparekraf.go.id/laporan-keuangan
- BPS Tourism Statistics (Statistik Wisatawan Mancanegara): https://www.bps.go.id

**Key figures to find**: average spend per tourist by country, length of stay, revenue from cultural vs. nature tourism.

---

### Problem 2: UMKM Digital Exclusion

**Claim to make**: The vast majority of traditional Indonesian artisan UMKM have no digital storefront or e-commerce presence.

**Where to research**:
- BPS Sensus Ekonomi 2016/2023: https://sensus.bps.go.id
- Kemenkop UMKM data reports: https://kemenkopukm.go.id/data-umkm
- ADB "Leveraging SMEs for Inclusive Growth" Southeast Asia: https://www.adb.org
- Google-Temasek-Bain e-Conomy SEA report: https://economysea.withgoogle.com

**Key figures to find**: number of UMKM in crafts/cultural sector, % with online presence, % in rural/semi-urban areas without internet access.

---

### Problem 3: Youth Disconnection from Cultural Heritage

**Claim to make**: Indonesian youth show declining awareness and interest in traditional kingdoms, folklore, and cultural practices.

**Where to research**:
- Kemendikbud (now Kemendikdasmen) surveys on cultural literacy: https://kemdikbud.go.id
- Pusat Data dan Statistik Pendidikan (PDSP): https://pusdatin.kemdikbud.go.id
- Academic papers: search Google Scholar for "Indonesian youth cultural identity" or "generasi muda warisan budaya Indonesia"
- UNESCO Jakarta youth culture reports: https://en.unesco.org/fieldoffice/jakarta

---

### Problem 4: UNESCO Heritage Sites — Count & Endangerment

**Claim to make**: Indonesia has X UNESCO World Heritage Sites, and many Indonesian cultural sites face risk from lack of digital documentation and tourism development.

**Where to research**:
- UNESCO World Heritage List: https://whc.unesco.org/en/list/ (filter by Indonesia — currently 9 sites as of 2024)
- BPCB (Balai Pelestarian Cagar Budaya) reports: https://kebudayaan.kemdikbud.go.id/bpcbjateng/
- Indonesian Ministry of Culture heritage risk assessments

**Key figures to find**: official count of UNESCO sites, count of national heritage sites (Cagar Budaya), percentage with digital documentation.

**How to research**:

### Problem 6: Artisan Income Levels

**Claim to make**: Traditional Indonesian craftspeople (batik, keris, wayang, tenun) earn below the regional minimum wage (UMR) and are leaving the trade due to low income.

**Where to research**:
- ILO Indonesia Country Reports: https://www.ilo.org/jakarta
- BPS Employment data by sector (Survei Angkatan Kerja Nasional / SAKERNAS): https://www.bps.go.id/subject/6/tenaga-kerja.html
- Kemenkop UMKM data on average artisan income: https://kemenkopukm.go.id
- Academic studies on Javanese batik or keris artisan income decline

---

### Problem 7: Tourism Concentration in Bali

**Claim to make**: Over 70% of international cultural tourism to Indonesia is concentrated in Bali, while 30+ other provinces with equally rich heritage receive minimal visitors.

**Where to research**:
- BPS Statistik Pariwisata Nusantara (domestic) and Mancanegara (international): https://www.bps.go.id/subject/16/pariwisata.html
- Kemenparekraf visitor statistics by province: https://kemenparekraf.go.id/statistik-kepariwisataan
- Bali Tourism Board reports

**Key figures to find**: % of total international arrivals landing in Bali, visitor numbers for Java/Sumatra/Sulawesi heritage sites vs. Bali.
